Re-collect is a social system where you write and rewrite -- to challenge impermanence.

Curious to take a look?

About

While every work you write stays permanently visible to you, others’ works would only be partially-visible to public by default. To immediately access others' works fully, you must rewrite it, and they must do the same for accessing your work. Each contribution in rewriting helps the work get more visible in public space. And all rewriting versions of a work collaboratively form a parallel interpretation by the community.

However, like memories, works in re-collect may partially fades away by time in public space, while each action of re-collecting a fragmental interpretation solidifies a work.

How disorienting would it be to interact on a platform where active involvement is required in order to access? How would you choose to collect — and re-collect when freed from illusion of permanence?

Just like real memories in life, collaborative interpretations in re-collect may be fragmental and imaginative, but through active involvements, we together challenge the impermanence nature.
